# Manufacturing ICP — Ideal Customer Profile

## Who to Target
- Ontario manufacturer, 20–500 employees, operating 10+ years (aging infrastructure)
- Sectors: automotive parts, food & beverage, plastics, metal fabrication, packaging,
  industrial equipment, aerospace, pharma
- Geography: Golden Horseshoe first (Milton, Hamilton, Burlington, Oakville, Guelph,
  Cambridge, Kitchener-Waterloo, Brantford), then broader Ontario
- No internal IT leadership visible (no CTO/CIO/IT Director on LinkedIn or website)

## Trigger Signals — AI-Specific (prioritise these)
- Publicly mentioned AI, automation, "Industry 4.0", or smart manufacturing
- Job posting for data analyst, automation engineer, or AI-related role — signals
  active interest without a governance strategy to support it
- Has deployed or is evaluating predictive maintenance, AI quality control, or
  AI-powered ERP features without an IT strategy connecting them
- Attended or presenting at manufacturing technology events (CMTS, Automate, etc.)

## Trigger Signals — General IT
- Recent plant expansion or second location (multi-site complexity)
- Supply chain compliance pressure from Tier 1 customer
- Cyber insurance renewal coming up (automotive, food, pharma especially)
- Legacy ERP (AS400, old on-prem system) — AI conversation opens the modernisation door

## Who to Contact
1. President / CEO / Owner (for <50 staff — owns the budget and the IT decision)
2. VP Operations / Director of Operations (feels the workflow pain most acutely)
3. CFO (cost angle: fixed-fee vs. break-fix, ROI of AI workflow automation)

## Primary Pain Points — Lead with These

### AI Strategy (highest urgency, best opening)
- **No coherent AI strategy**: Different departments are deploying AI tools independently
  — production wants workflow automation, finance wants reporting, leadership wants
  insights — with no governance connecting them. One unvetted tool on the shop floor
  network is a cyber insurance coverage gap.
- **AI governance for cyber insurance**: Insurers are beginning to ask about AI
  governance policies in renewals, just as they started asking about cybersecurity
  frameworks two years ago. Getting ahead of this is easier before renewal.
- **Government incentives require a documented strategy**: Federal and provincial
  AI adoption and modernisation incentives are available to manufacturers, but
  accessing them requires a documented technology roadmap — something most don't have.
- **OT/IT/AI convergence**: AI-powered tools on the shop floor (predictive maintenance,
  quality control cameras, AI-enhanced ERP) are network-connected but governed separately
  from office IT. Nobody owns the AI governance piece that spans both.

### Secondary Pain Points
- Supply chain compliance, cyber insurance readiness, ERP modernisation, multi-site
  network design, fractional CIO, no internal IT leadership

## Research Approach
- LinkedIn company search: Ontario + manufacturing sector + employee count
- Ontario Chamber of Commerce, CME (Canadian Manufacturers & Exporters) directories
- Google: "[company name] AI" or "[company name] automation" for any public signals
- Company website: "About" and "News" pages for technology initiatives
- Job postings: data analyst, automation engineer, IT roles (presence or absence both signal)
- Trade press: Canadian Plastics, Canadian Metalworking, Food in Canada for sector news

## Outreach Hooks — AI Strategy Led

**Hook 1 (no coherent strategy — most common)**
"Most manufacturers we're talking to right now are in the same spot — AI is landing
on the agenda from four directions at once (production, IT, finance, leadership) with
no coherent plan connecting them. We've built a structured AI strategy engagement
specifically for manufacturers navigating this: vendor-agnostic, fixed-fee, and it
produces documents you can act on — not a slide deck."

**Hook 2 (cyber insurance angle)**
"Cyber insurers are starting to ask about AI governance the same way they started
asking about cybersecurity frameworks a few years ago. Manufacturers that get ahead
of this before renewal are in a better position. We've helped a few Ontario manufacturers
build exactly this kind of documentation."

**Hook 3 (government incentives)**
"There are federal and provincial AI modernisation incentives available to manufacturers
right now — but accessing them requires a documented technology roadmap. Most operations
teams are too busy running production to write one. That's the kind of thing we can
help with in a fixed-scope engagement."

**Hook 4 (OT/IT convergence — for manufacturers with shop floor tech)**
"The AI tools showing up on the shop floor — predictive maintenance platforms, quality
control systems, AI-enhanced ERP features — are increasingly network-connected but
governed separately from office IT. We work with manufacturers to close that gap before
it becomes a security or insurance issue."
