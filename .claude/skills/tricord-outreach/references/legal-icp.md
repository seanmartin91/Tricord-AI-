# Legal ICP — Ideal Customer Profile

## Who to Target
- Ontario law firm, 5–100 lawyers
- Practice areas: personal injury, family, real estate, corporate/commercial,
  wills & estates, employment, immigration, general practice
- Using or evaluating: Actionstep, Filevine, Clio, PCLaw
- Managing Partner still making IT and AI decisions (no dedicated IT staff)

## Trigger Signals — AI-Specific (prioritise these)
- Firm has publicly mentioned AI tools, Copilot, ChatGPT, or "legal AI" on their
  website, blog, or LinkedIn — they're thinking about it but probably have no policy
- Law firm using Microsoft 365 / Copilot without a governance framework in place
- No IT Director or CISO listed — AI decisions land on the Managing Partner by default
- Firm grew through merger — two separate IT environments = two sets of AI tools,
  no unified governance
- Job posting for "legal tech" or "knowledge management" — signals active AI interest

## Trigger Signals — General IT
- On-prem server infrastructure (no cloud mention on website)
- Using Actionstep or Filevine (Tricord is certified Actionstep partner — warm door)
- Recently expanded practice areas or opened a new office

## Who to Contact
1. Managing Partner (owns strategy, feels liability and law society risk, controls budget)
2. COO / Practice Manager / Firm Administrator (day-to-day operations, often the
   champion who brings the Managing Partner in)

## Primary Pain Points — Lead with These

### AI Governance (highest urgency)
- **Shadow AI already in play**: Associates are using ChatGPT, Copilot, and Grammarly
  on client files right now — without IT approval and without a privilege risk assessment.
  This is factual at virtually every firm. It's the sharpest opening hook.
- **Law Society pressure**: Provincial law societies have issued AI guidance. Most
  managing partners haven't translated that guidance into a firm-level policy. The gap
  is visible and creates liability.
- **US CLOUD Act / Canadian data residency**: Many AI platforms (including major ones)
  process data in US data centres. The CLOUD Act means US authorities can compel access
  regardless of physical storage location. For Canadian clients this is a real exposure.
- **The Copilot demo problem**: Firms get impressive demos but have no idea whether
  client data stays in Canada or what Microsoft's data handling actually means for them.
- **No policy before the incident**: The risk is a data incident or law society audit
  that exposes the firm had no AI governance in place.

### Secondary Pain Points
- Actionstep/Filevine reporting gaps, cyber insurance readiness, M365 migration,
  fractional CIO (Managing Partner spending 20% of time on IT decisions)

## Research Approach
- Law Society of Ontario directory (lso.ca) — searchable by city and practice area
- Firm websites: team page (spot the IT gap), blog/news for any AI mentions
- LinkedIn: recent posts about Copilot, ChatGPT, legal AI, or technology investment
- Google: "[firm name] AI" or "[firm name] technology" for any public statements
- Job boards: "legal tech", "knowledge management", "IT coordinator" postings

## Hunter.io Strategy for Law Firms
Law firms often use firstname@firmname.ca or firstname.lastname@firmname.com.
Target Managing Partner or Firm Administrator specifically.
If no email found, note "LinkedIn outreach only" — don't skip the firm.

## Outreach Hooks — AI Strategy Led

**Hook 1 (shadow AI — strongest)**
"Most managing partners we talk to have found out — sometimes by accident — that their
associates are using ChatGPT or Copilot on client files without a policy in place. We
built a three-lane AI governance framework specifically to help law firms get ahead of
that before it becomes a law society issue."

**Hook 2 (data residency)**
"The AI tools getting the most attention in legal — Copilot, Harvey, contract review
platforms — almost all process data through US infrastructure by default. The CLOUD Act
means that's not just a privacy question; it's a data sovereignty question for client
files. Happy to talk through what that means for [Firm]'s specific practice areas."

**Hook 3 (Actionstep/Filevine — warm)**
"We're a certified Actionstep partner. Most firms on the platform don't know about the
reporting and AI integration capabilities it's added in the last 12 months — and the
governance questions those features raise are ones we've already helped firms work through."

**Hook 4 (law society guidance)**
"The Law Society guidance on AI is driving a lot of calls right now. Most managing
partners want a governance framework that's defensible — not a generic policy copied
from somewhere else. We've built this specifically for Ontario firms."
