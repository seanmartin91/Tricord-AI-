# Additional Sectors — ICP & AI Outreach Angles

These sectors share Tricord's core value proposition (vendor-agnostic AI governance,
Canadian data residency, senior-led, fixed-fee) and are actively facing AI adoption
pressure with no internal IT leadership to manage it.

---

## 1. CPA / Accounting Firms

### Who to Target
- Ontario CPA firms, 5–80 staff
- Offering: audit, tax, advisory, bookkeeping, corporate accounting
- Managing Partner or Senior Partner still making technology decisions

### Why Now (AI trigger)
- AI is transforming audit and bookkeeping faster than any other professional service —
  tools like Caseware AI, Karbon AI, QuickBooks AI, and Copilot for Finance are actively
  being demoed to partners
- CPA Ontario has issued guidance on AI use in public practice — most small firms
  haven't operationalised it into a policy
- Client financial data carries the same Canadian residency obligations as legal files
- The "shadow AI" problem is identical to law firms: staff are using ChatGPT to draft
  financial memos and analyse client data without a governance framework

### Trigger Signals
- No IT Director visible — partner or office manager making technology calls
- Using cloud accounting platforms (Xero, QBO, Caseware) — AI features being added
- Firm has grown through merger or acquisition (two environments, no unified governance)
- Job posting for "technology coordinator" or "systems administrator"

### Who to Contact
1. Managing Partner / Senior Partner (strategy and budget)
2. Firm Administrator / COO (operations)

### Pain Points (AI-led)
- **CPA Ontario guidance on AI** — similar to law society situation; guidance issued,
  policy translation gap at the firm level
- **Client financial data residency** — tax files, audit files, financial statements
  containing client confidential data going into US-hosted AI tools
- **Audit trail and AI output verification** — AI-generated draft financial content
  needs human review standards; no framework means no defensibility at audit
- **Vendor lock-in risk** — accounting software vendors are bundling AI into existing
  subscriptions; firms are adopting by default, not by policy

### Outreach Hook
"Accounting software vendors are quietly adding AI to existing subscriptions — Xero,
QuickBooks, Caseware. Most CPA firms are adopting these features by default rather than
by policy, and client financial data is going through those tools without a governance
framework in place. We help practices get ahead of that before it becomes a CPA Ontario
issue."

## 2. Pharmaceutical / Life Sciences

### Who to Target
- Ontario pharma, biotech, contract research organisations (CROs), medical device
  manufacturers, nutraceutical companies
- 20–500 employees
- Often located in: Mississauga, Oakville, Burlington, Markham, Kitchener-Waterloo
- Health Canada regulated; may also face FDA/ICH requirements for export markets

### Why Now (AI trigger)
- AI is entering drug discovery, clinical trial design, regulatory submission writing,
  pharmacovigilance, and quality management — all touching regulated data
- Health Canada's GxP regulations (Good Manufacturing Practice, Good Clinical Practice)
  are increasingly scrutinised for AI-generated content in submissions
- Supply chain AI tools (demand forecasting, inventory optimisation) are being added
  without governance frameworks
- Canadian data residency matters acutely — clinical trial data, patient data, and
  Health Canada submission data cannot be freely processed in US data centres

### Trigger Signals
- No CISO or IT Director visible — VP of Operations or QA Director making IT calls
- Job posting for data scientist, bioinformatics, or AI/ML engineer
- Recent regulatory approval or new product launch (IT complexity increases fast)
- Mentioned "AI" or "machine learning" in a press release without referencing governance
- CRO or CDMO relationship (contract work creates complex data-sharing governance needs)

### Who to Contact
1. VP Operations / COO (owns the IT and compliance integration gap)
2. VP Quality / QA Director (understands GxP, most receptive to governance framing)
3. CEO/President at smaller (<50 staff) biotechs

### Pain Points (AI-led)
- **GxP + AI validation**: Health Canada GxP requires validated systems — AI tools
  used in regulated processes need qualification and audit trails most companies lack
- **Clinical data residency**: Patient and trial data cannot be processed through
  US-hosted AI without specific contractual and jurisdictional protections
- **Regulatory submission AI**: Pharma companies are using AI to draft regulatory
  submissions — Health Canada's position on AI-generated content is still evolving,
  making governance documentation more important, not less
- **Shadow AI in R&D**: Scientists using publicly available AI tools on proprietary
  compound data, clinical results, or formulation information — IP leakage risk

### Outreach Hook
"Health Canada GxP regulations require validated systems — and AI tools are increasingly
being used in regulated processes (QMS, pharmacovigilance, regulatory submissions)
without the validation documentation those processes require. We've built AI governance
frameworks specifically for regulated environments, where the audit trail and defensibility
requirements are fundamentally different from standard enterprise IT."

## 3. Engineering & Architecture Firms (AEC)

### Who to Target
- Ontario engineering consultancies, architecture firms, environmental consultancies
- 10–200 staff
- Disciplines: structural, civil, mechanical, electrical, environmental, geotechnical,
  building architecture
- Often: no dedicated IT leadership; principal engineers making technology calls

### Why Now (AI trigger)
- AI design tools (Autodesk AI, Revit AI, generative design) are actively being adopted
- AI is being used for code compliance checking, environmental assessment, report
  generation — all producing work product with professional liability implications
- PEO (Professional Engineers Ontario) and OAA (Ontario Association of Architects)
  have not yet issued comprehensive AI guidance — but liability risk is real
- Federal and provincial infrastructure spending is creating project volume; firms
  are using AI to handle capacity, often without governance

### Trigger Signals
- Using Autodesk, Bentley, or Revit products (AI being added to these platforms)
- Rapid growth in headcount (hiring = AI tool sprawl without governance)
- Working on government or municipal contracts (data handling requirements increasing)
- Job posting for BIM coordinator, data analyst, or technology roles

### Who to Contact
1. Principal / Managing Principal (owns liability and strategy)
2. Studio/Practice Director
3. COO or Business Operations Director (if firm has one)

### Pain Points (AI-led)
- **Professional liability + AI output**: PEO stamps and OAA seals attach professional
  liability to work product. If AI generated or influenced the analysis, what review
  standard applies? Most firms have no policy.
- **Client data and project IP**: Client site data, proprietary designs, and project
  financials going through AI tools without data handling agreements
- **Government project compliance**: Infrastructure Ontario and federal projects
  increasingly require documented data governance for contractors
- **Tool sprawl**: Principals are independently adopting different AI tools; no unified
  framework means no unified liability posture

### Outreach Hook
"AI design tools are making engineers more productive — but PEO and OAA professional
liability still attaches to every stamped deliverable, whether AI was in the workflow
or not. Most Ontario engineering and architecture firms we've spoken with haven't
established the review discipline or documentation standards that protect them if an
AI-influenced deliverable is questioned. That's a governance gap we can help close
before a project creates the issue."

## 4. Insurance Brokerages & MGAs

### Who to Target
- Ontario insurance brokerages and managing general agencies (MGAs)
- 10–150 staff
- RIBO (Registered Insurance Brokers of Ontario) regulated
- Offering personal lines, commercial lines, specialty lines, or life & benefits

### Why Now (AI trigger)
- AI tools are being adopted for claims triage, risk assessment, client communication,
  and policy comparison — all touching policyholder data
- FSRA (Financial Services Regulatory Authority of Ontario) is actively developing
  AI governance guidance for financial services
- Insurers are beginning to ask MGAs and brokers about AI governance as part of
  carrier appointment and E&O coverage requirements
- Broker management systems (Applied Epic, Acturis, Vertafore) are adding AI features

### Trigger Signals
- No IT Director or CTO visible
- Using Applied Epic, Acturis, or Vertafore (AI features being embedded)
- Growing headcount or book of business (scaling creates data governance pressure)
- Carrier or E&O renewal coming up (AI governance documentation increasingly expected)
- Job posting for "data analyst" or "technology coordinator"

### Who to Contact
1. President / Principal Broker (owns RIBO compliance and strategy)
2. COO or Operations Manager
3. Director of Commercial Lines or Personal Lines (where AI adoption is fastest)

### Pain Points (AI-led)
- **Policyholder data through US AI tools**: PIPEDA and Ontario privacy obligations
  apply; policyholder data fed into US-hosted AI without adequate data processing
  agreements creates FSRA exposure
- **FSRA AI guidance**: FSRA is actively developing guidance for financial services
  firms using AI in client-facing processes — getting governance in place ahead of
  final guidance is easier than retrofitting after
- **E&O and carrier requirements**: Errors and omissions insurers are beginning to
  ask about AI governance as part of renewal; brokers with documented policies are
  in a better position
- **Applied Epic / Acturis AI features**: BMS vendors adding AI to existing platforms;
  brokers adopting by default without governance or training standards

### Outreach Hook
"FSRA is actively developing AI governance guidance for Ontario financial services firms —
and E&O insurers are starting to ask about AI governance at renewal, the same way cyber
insurers started asking about cybersecurity frameworks a few years ago. Insurance
brokerages that document their AI governance posture now are ahead of that curve. We've
built a fixed-fee engagement specifically for regulated professional services firms that
produces the documentation you'd need."

## 5. Healthcare / Allied Health Groups

### Who to Target
- Ontario private healthcare: specialist clinics, diagnostic imaging, physiotherapy
  groups, dental groups (multi-location), mental health networks, optometry chains
- 15–300 staff
- PHIPA (Personal Health Information Protection Act) regulated
- Often: practice manager or business owner making IT decisions, no dedicated IT

### Why Now (AI trigger)
- AI is being integrated into EMR/EHR platforms (Oscar, PS Suite, Accuro), diagnostic
  imaging (AI-assisted radiology), scheduling, and clinical documentation
- PHIPA creates explicit requirements for where health information is stored and
  processed — US-hosted AI is a compliance risk most clinic owners haven't evaluated
- Ontario Health's digital health strategy is pushing interoperability and data
  connectivity — increasing the governance complexity
- AI scribe tools (Nuance DAX, AI medical scribes) are being adopted rapidly by
  physicians without a PHIPA-compliant data handling framework

### Trigger Signals
- Multi-location practice (IT complexity scales with locations)
- Using an EMR that is adding AI features (Oscar Pro, Accuro, PS Suite)
- No IT Director visible — practice manager or administrator making technology calls
- Recent clinic acquisition or group formation (two systems, no unified governance)
- Using or evaluating AI scribe / clinical documentation tools

### Who to Contact
1. Practice Owner / Medical Director (clinical and business accountability)
2. Practice Manager / Clinic Administrator (day-to-day IT decision maker)
3. COO (for larger multi-location groups)

### Pain Points (AI-led)
- **PHIPA + AI**: Personal health information processed through US-hosted AI tools
  without an appropriate data processing agreement is a PHIPA violation — most clinic
  owners don't know their AI scribe or scheduling tool's data residency
- **AI scribe adoption**: Physicians are adopting AI scribes (Nuance DAX, Suki,
  ambient clinical AI) faster than governance is catching up — patient conversations
  being transcribed and processed through US cloud infrastructure
- **IPC oversight**: Ontario's Information and Privacy Commissioner is actively
  scrutinising health sector AI governance; documented compliance posture matters
- **EMR AI features**: Vendors adding AI to existing EMR subscriptions — clinics
  adopting by default, not by policy

### Outreach Hook
"AI medical scribes and EMR AI features are making clinicians more efficient — but
PHIPA treats patient health information processed through US-hosted AI the same way
it treats any offshore data transfer. Most clinic owners we've spoken with are surprised
to find out their AI scribe or scheduling assistant isn't covered by their existing PHIPA
risk assessment. We help multi-site healthcare groups close that gap with a structured,
fixed-fee engagement that produces documentation you can show to a PHIPA audit."
