## The Three-Lane Governance Model (use this as a differentiator)

Tricord separates AI into three distinct lanes — this prevents one team's productivity
tool from becoming another team's compliance incident:

- **Lane 1 — Client-Confidential AI**: Tools touching client/patient/regulated data.
  Highest scrutiny. Data residency, confidentiality controls, human oversight required.
- **Lane 2 — Business AI**: Operations, admin, communications (Copilot, Power Automate).
  Lower risk but still needs policy — acceptable use, data handling, output review.
- **Lane 3 — Infrastructure AI**: IT automation, security monitoring, anomaly detection.
  Managed by Tricord as part of Fractional CIO / managed IT services.

This three-lane framing is concrete and memorable. Use it in outreach when space allows.

## For Law Firms — AI Governance Outreach

### The single sharpest hook
"Your lawyers are probably already using AI on client files — ChatGPT, Grammarly,
Copilot — without IT approval. That's a privilege issue waiting to happen."

Shadow AI is already in play at virtually every firm. This is factual, urgent, and
immediately relevant to the managing partner's liability concerns.

### Secondary hooks
- **Law Society guidance**: Ontario and other provincial law societies have issued AI
  guidance that most managing partners haven't translated into a firm-level policy yet.
  The clock is ticking.
- **US CLOUD Act + Canadian data residency**: Many AI platforms process data in US
  data centres by default. The CLOUD Act means US authorities can compel access
  regardless of where data is physically stored. For firms serving Canadian clients,
  this is a real exposure — not theoretical.
- **The Copilot demo problem**: "We got a Copilot demo. It looked impressive. But we
  have no idea what happens to our data or whether it stays in Canada." Tricord helps
  firms answer exactly this question before deploying.
- **Vendor-agnostic**: Most AI advice comes from vendors selling a tool. Tricord has
  no referral relationships — the recommendation reflects the firm's needs only.

### What to avoid mentioning in cold outreach
- Solicitor-client privilege in technical legal terms (too specific for a cold email)
- Trust accounting (too narrow for initial hook)
- Keep it at the managing partner level: risk, liability, governance, defensibility

### Personalisation angles
- If the firm has publicly mentioned AI adoption or AI tools → reference directly
- If the firm uses Copilot/M365 → "you may already be using AI without a governance
  framework in place"
- If the firm recently merged → "two separate IT environments usually means two
  separate sets of AI tools being used independently, with no unified policy"
- If firm is in personal injury, immigration, or family law → heavier Canadian client
  data exposure to reference

## For Manufacturers — AI Strategy Outreach

### The primary hook
"AI is moving from hype to operational reality — but most manufacturers are deploying
tools department by department with no governance connecting them."

The risk for manufacturers is operational and insurance-related, not privilege-based:
a single unvetted AI tool accessing production data can create a cyber insurance
coverage dispute, a supply chain compliance gap, or a data leak.

### Secondary hooks
- **No coherent strategy**: Different departments want different things from AI.
  Production wants workflow automation. Finance wants reporting. Leadership wants
  insights. Nobody is coordinating.
- **Government modernisation incentives**: Federal and provincial AI adoption incentives
  are available — but accessing them requires a documented strategy.
- **OT/IT AI convergence**: AI-powered tools on the shop floor (predictive maintenance,
  quality control cameras) are increasingly network-connected and IT-governed. Nobody
  owns the AI governance piece that connects them.
- **Cyber insurance**: Insurers are beginning to ask about AI governance in policy
  renewals — just as they did with cybersecurity frameworks two years ago.

### Personalisation angles
- If manufacturer has published anything about AI, automation, or Industry 4.0 → lead
  with that specific initiative
- If manufacturer is in a regulated sector (food safety, aerospace, pharma) → data
  governance angle is strongest
- If manufacturer has recently grown or expanded → "at your scale, the AI strategy
  question usually moves from 'should we?' to 'how do we govern it?' fast"
